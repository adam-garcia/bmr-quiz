scheduling your run club

Schools can implement run clubs all year long or for shorter durations.
What do you think would work best for your school?
When will your run club take place?
How many days a week will your run club take place?
It is recommended that kids get 30 minutes of physical activity a day at school. When designing your schedule, consider all the opportunities your students have to be active and how adding a run club can help your school meet this target.
How long will your run club sessions be?
Consider this when planning your run club: kids need at least 60 minutes/day of physical activity, mostly at moderate or vigorous intensity. It’s recommended that at least 30 of those minutes happen at school.
Where will students walk & run?
Remember your go-to location doesn’t need to be your only location. Mixing up where you run club takes place is a great way to introduce variety into your routine.

organizing your run club

Use this space to write down the names of colleagues, parents, and administrators who will assist with your run club.
How will miles be tracked? Some run clubs track students' individual miles while others estimate miles taht students run as a group
  • Many schools use an app like MapMyRun to determine how many laps around their track/soccer field/school perimeter is equal to one mile. Once you know this you can use any of a number of systems to count student laps. We’ve seen schools use punch cards, popsicle sticks, dots on a card and more.
  • If you have access to pedometers, counting steps is a simple way to track miles. You can use our calculator to convert steps to miles whether you have pedometers for all your students or you select one or two to wear them and represent a group.
  • Tracking how long your students walk and run for is handy way to estimate miles. Our calculator will do the math for you!
Which students will take part?
While we want to see as many students in your school taking part as possible, phasing a run club class by class or grade by grade can help lay a foundation for success.

making it fun

How will you make your run club for students and celebrate successs
  • Stickers, charms, medals, and t shirts are always popular but don’t be afraid to get creative. An extra recess, a homework pass, or even a Principal doing something silly makes for a great reward.
  • Give shout outs to run club successes in your morning announcements, in your school newsletter, on your website and social media channels, or at school assemblies.
  • When run club means socializing and hip music, kids will want to be there.
